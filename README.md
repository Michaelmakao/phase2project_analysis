# phase2project_analysis
Analyzing films that perform best at boxoffice and rt.reviews for a new movie studio
# Movie Studio Data Analysis

# about the Project
This project was developed as part of a business case study for a new movie studio initiative. The company is exploring how to break into the competitive film industry — and they’ve asked for help understanding what kinds of movies tend to perform best at the box office.
Using two datasets — one with box office performance, and another with film critic reviews — this analysis identifies patterns in genre, revenue, and critical response to help inform smart content decisions.

# Datasets Used

1. **Box Office Dataset (`box_office`)**
   - Contains data on movie titles, studios, domestic/foreign gross, total gross, and release year.
2. **Rotten Tomatoes Reviews Dataset (`rt_clean`)**
   - Includes critic reviews, review ratings, sentiment (fresh or rotten), publisher, and reviewer details.

##  Key Questions We Explored

- What kinds of movies earn the most money?
- Which studios dominate box office performance?
- How do critics generally feel about recent films?
- Which critics are the most active or influential?

## Key Findings

- **Studios like Disney and Warner Bros lead** in total gross revenue, thanks to major franchises and high production value.
- **Total revenue varies by year**, with noticeable spikes in blockbuster-heavy periods.
- **Critics are generally positive**, with most reviews labeled as “fresh.”
- A handful of critics dominate the conversation some names appear in hundreds of reviews.

## Business Recommendations
Based on the patterns that I observed the company should :
- **Focus on original content that appeals to mainstream audiences**, while keeping production quality high especially for action, adventure, or animated genres.
- **Time releases around high-performing seasons** like summer and winter holidays.
- **Engage with top critics** and reputable review platforms early in the marketing cycle to build momentum.
- **Benchmark performance against top studios** their success models can guide your studio's creative and distribution strategies.
